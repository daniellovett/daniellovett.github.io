By evaluating the trends in income for each segment, it becomes clear that the idea of "the shrinking middle class" is only true based on the arguably flawed definition of the "middle class" (the range from two-thirds of the median income to two times the median income).

It is clear that while the mean income and share of aggregate income have consistently increased for one-fifth of households, the remainder of the country's households has not seen this same level of progress. Further research could compare changes in productivity attributed to each segment to determine if the apparent disparity can be explained by orthodox economic principles. The linguistic and rhetorical effects of characterizing income inequality as "the shrinking middle class" could also be explored.
