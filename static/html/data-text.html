From 1967 to 2019, the share of aggregate income in the United States held by the highest earning one-fifth of households increased by 18.8% while decreasing for the other four-fifths of households. The decrease in share of aggregate income was more stark in the bottom 40% of earners.

During the same period, a similar disparity can be seen in the changes to mean income for each fifth of the country's households. While the mean income for the wealthiest one-fifth of households has more than doubled since 1967, it has only increased by 36.6 to 67.2% for the remainder of households.
